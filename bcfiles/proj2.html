Modern Minimalistic Desk

A custom hand-builted two-piece table with a modern minimalistic design paired with a quick disassembly system for ease of transportation.

When it comes to the desk I use on a daily, I have very specific requirements. First it must be large, at a minimum of 6x2 feet. Second, it should be modular in order to add any future upgrades or simply for ease of transportation. Third, it must be aesthetically pleasing.

I set out to build my current computer desk from scratch. The table is made of plywood and filled with resin to the core. The top of the table rests on a C-shaped table leg with attaches to the table with 4 bolts.

Completed Table

This is an image of the table completed and installed.

Wood Assembly

At the core of the table is a closed wooden box made of plywood. Plywood is a cheap median for wood that is not only strong but also fairly light. Wood glue and screws have been used to keep the pieces together.

Resin

Clear glossy resin is mixed with a flat white dye to produce a gloss white finish. Each layer was about 1/8 inch thick. Over a dozen layers were poured with each layer taking up to 4 hours to dry.

Touch-Up

With the layers of resin reaching the top of the table, additional layers are poured for the resin to flow over the edges and coat the sides of the table as well. This produces a even finish along each side of the table. However, the resin leaves drip marks on the bottom edge that needs to be sanded off.
